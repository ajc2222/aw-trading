Chart Visual Prototypes

5 alternatives to replace the indicator card chart — all use existing CSS variables and no external dependencies.

Option A Current
Original Candles
White-to-blue gradient · grey down candles

The existing implementation. Issues: wicks appear disconnected, white-top looks washed out, down candles are nearly invisible against the dark card.

Option B Recommended
Glowing Area Chart
SVG path · gradient fill · subtle glow stroke

Best for visual impact. Smooth uptrend conveys growth at a glance. The glow stroke and pulsing dot on the current price feel alive without being noisy. Scales well on mobile.

Option C
Trading Candles
Green / red · proportional wicks · professional

Most recognisable to traders. Classic green/red palette traders know immediately. Wicks are properly positioned relative to body height. Down candles are clearly readable.

Option D
Momentum Bars
No wicks · wide bars · minimal clutter

Cleanest read at small size. Removing wicks eliminates the visual noise. Blue gradient up-bars are bold and brand-consistent. Hover brightens all bars for interactivity.

Option E
Sparkline
Smooth SVG curve · trade signal dots

Tells a story. Smooth curve shows the trend clearly. Green dots represent trade entries — directly communicates the indicator's purpose. Most unique visual vs competitor sites.
