Module 1 · Week 1
Why Price Does What It Does
Film Thu Jul 10
Topics
  • Institutional order flow — the actual driver of price
  • Why retail setups fail (and what's really happening)
  • How to read a move: delivery, expansion, retracement
  • The difference between reacting and anticipating
  • Reading context — what came before matters more than the pattern
    • Why it's first
    Most traders memorize setups without understanding the force behind them. This module reframes how students see the chart — they stop looking for patterns and start reading intent. Everything else in the curriculum builds on this mental model.
    Module 2 · Week 2
    Building Your Data Collection System
    Film Thu Jul 17
    Topics
  • What data actually matters (and what's noise)
  • How to log a trade: not just entry/exit but the read
  • Tracking setups by session, instrument, time of day
  • Weekly review ritual — what to look for in your own data
  • How to turn a loss into useful information
    • Why it's second
    You can't build an edge you haven't measured. This module gives students the system to start collecting their own data immediately — so by month 3, they have real information about how they specifically perform in the market.
    Module 3 · Week 3
    Finding Your Edge in the Data
    Film Thu Jul 24
    Topics
  • How to review a month of trades and find your real patterns
  • When to stop trading a setup that isn't working for you
  • Identifying your highest-probability conditions
  • The difference between a market edge and a personal edge
  • How to narrow your playbook based on what your data says
    • Why it's third
    Most traders try to copy someone else's edge. This module teaches students to extract their own from their own data — so the edge is theirs, not borrowed. This is what turns someone from a student into a trader.
    Module 4 · Week 4
    Psychology — Understanding Yourself as the Variable
    Film Thu Jul 31
    Topics
  • Why you deviate from your plan — and what that reveals
  • Recognizing your personal tilt triggers
  • The pre-trade mental checklist (not rules — awareness)
  • How to process a losing streak without destroying your edge
  • Self-honesty: the gap between what you think you do and what you actually do
    • Why it's fourth
    By module 4, students have data on their own behavior. This module helps them interpret it honestly. The goal isn't a rigid rule set — it's self-awareness so they can catch themselves before the damage happens.
    Module 5 · Week 5
    Building Discretion — Reading What the Market Is Telling You
    Film Thu Aug 7
    Topics
  • What discretion actually means (it's not guessing)
  • How to read confluence without a checklist
  • When the setup is valid but the read says no
  • Developing conviction — how to know when you actually have edge
  • The role of patience in discretionary trading
    • Why it's fifth
    After 4 modules of self-study and data collection, students are ready to move beyond rules. This is the hardest module to teach — it's about judgment, not knowledge. Delivered through chart walkthroughs and real examples, not definitions.
    Module 6 · Week 6
    Advanced Concepts — What I've Actually Picked Up
    Film Thu Aug 14
    Topics
  • Advanced concepts beyond standard ICT (your own observations)
  • What you've learned from losing trades specifically
  • How you read sessions differently now vs. when you started
  • Market behaviors that aren't widely talked about
  • How to keep evolving — your learning process post-mentorship
    • Why it's last
    This is the "here's what I've actually figured out" module — your real edge shared directly. It lands best last because students have the context to understand it. It's also the most unique content you can offer — nobody else has this.
