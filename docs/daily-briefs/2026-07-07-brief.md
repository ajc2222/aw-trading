## A) YouTube Video Outline — "What is ICT and Why Does It Actually Work?"

**Hook (first 30 sec — word for word):**
> "Most traders lose because they trade against the wrong people. Today I'm showing you exactly who's on the other side of your trades, and how ICT concepts let you read what they're doing before price moves."

**Timestamps and talking points:**

- **0:00 – 0:45 | Hook** (above) — deliver with energy, straight to camera, no warm-up.
- **0:45 – 3:00 | What ICT actually is** — institutional order flow, not a retail indicator or signal service. Frame it as "reading the footprints of the people who actually move price" — banks, funds, algorithms — versus retail patterns that get hunted.
- **3:00 – 6:00 | Concept 1: Market structure** — higher highs/higher lows vs. lower highs/lower lows. Show on-screen: a clean chart with recent swing highs and lows marked, explain who's "in control" at each stage.
- **6:00 – 9:00 | Concept 2: Liquidity (buy-side/sell-side)** — where retail stops cluster (above swing highs, below swing lows) and why price is drawn to those levels before reversing. Show on-screen: a liquidity sweep — price running stops, then reversing hard.
- **9:00 – 12:00 | Concept 3: Order blocks** — the last opposing candle before a strong displacement move; institutional footprint. Show on-screen: an order block chart with the displacement candle highlighted.
- **12:00 – 14:00 | Live chart: all three combined** — walk through one fully annotated real example showing structure, a liquidity grab, and the order block that triggered the move, in sequence.
- **14:00 – end | Closing CTA (exact words):**
  > "If this clicked for you, I break down setups like this daily in my free community — link's in the description. Come learn to read price the way the institutions actually trade it."
  Always end on the Discord link.

**Chart examples to have pre-marked before filming:**
- Clean market structure chart (swing highs/lows labeled)
- Liquidity sweep chart (stop hunt then reversal)
- Order block chart (displacement candle highlighted)
- One combined example tying all three together
